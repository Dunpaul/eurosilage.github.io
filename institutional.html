PROJECT OVERVIEW
  • According to data from ministry of Agriculture department of feed & Nutrition, Kenya requires 55 million metric tonnes of animal feeds annually.
  • However, the current production stand at only 22 million metric tonnes leaving a supply gap of 33 million metric tonnes annually.
  • Research also shows that 90% of crop remains especially maize is wasted after harvest as farmers lack knowledge on feed processing & preservation.
The main reasons for the massive animal feed supply gap are:
  • Most of the crop remains suitable for feed or fodder is wasted after crop is harvested.
  • Majority of Kenyan farmers lack knowledge of feed processing & preservation.
  • The few farmers who preserve fodder use the old technology like pit silos.
  • Research by USAID shows farmers loose up to 37% of fodder due to lack of correct preservation knowledge.
PROBLEM STATEMENT
  • How can we increase yield from animals with limited grazing field without using tradition feed conserving method?
  • Domestic animals give us food and income, a better fed animal will give better yields.
  • The current costs of animal feeds is high for most farmers making livestock farming an unprofitable venture.
  • The impact of climate change has resulted to unpredictable weather patterns which has affected fodder crop growth.
WAYFORWAD
  • Recent research indicate that an acre of maize field can produce 18-25 tonnes of silage. This with a dry matter (DM) of 30% can feed 6 cows for 3 months with recommended consumption of 2% of its body weight.
  • The cost of producing silage is also relatively low at 2 Ksh. per kg during wet season & Ksh. 4.5 during the dry season.

Therefore,

  • Our farmers need to be impacted with latest feed Processing & preservation technology for sustainable production.
  • Through training and provision of required processing technology, we can drastically reduce the acreage required to sustain a cow.
  • That is why Euro silage Ltd is providing cost effective and modern technology of processing and storing forage for up to two years in affordable silage bags.
WHY SILAGE MASTER 3000?
  • We are introducing affordable technology of processing and storing forage for up to two years using affordable silage bags thus guaranteeing the farmer availability of feeds throughout the two years.
  • Our silage processing plant called the ‘silage master 3000’ has a capacity to process 5,000 – 8,000 kgs of silage per hour, translating to 50,000 – 80,000 kgs a day.
  • Silage master can process maize silage, wheat silage, grass, alfa alfa silage, sugar beet pulp, nappier grass, sorghum, crop remains and any other type of animal feeds which can be chopped and any type of fruits and vegetables like potatoes, tomatoes and market waste vegetable materials.
  • During the rainy season you process the silage at Kshs. 2.5 per Kg that can later be sold at an affordable price of less than Kshs. 25 per Kg, thus reducing the cost of feeding the animals at all seasons.
  • As a profitable business venture it can process various fodder crops at a relatively cheap price of between Ksh. 2.5 to Ksh. 4.5 per kilo selling between Ksh 10 - 15 per kilo therefore generating at least Ksh 750,000 a day.
HOW THE SILAGE MASTER 3000 WORKS
  • The plant cuts and chops fodder material into small pieces of 3cm long.
  • They are then transferred and mixed with other nutrients in a rotating bunker to make silage.
  • The silage is filled into plastic bags between 25 to 50kg which is convenient to store, use and transport.
  • The silage is then pressed and compacted using 8 bar pneumatic air cylinders at 6,000 kgs pressure.
  • Thereafter, the silage is vacuumed to remove air to create anaerobic condition for essential bacteria growth.
  • Then the bags are double sealed to prevent air leaks and thereafter stored ready for use after 15 days and or preserved for a period of up-to two years.
  • It is an automated system with Plc window 7” touch screen to control and operate the plant.
  • With ethernet port to enable remote access of the plant from anywhere.

Advantages of silage over traditional fodder conservation.
  • Increases feeding appetite of animals.
  • Uniform milk production throughout the year.
  • Forage for silage only requires 30% dry matter & 70% moisture content.
  • Crops can be harvested at optimal phase of development.
  • Better use of land with 2-3 crops annually.
  • Longer shelf life of up-to 2 years
  • Better stock management.
  • 2kg of silage (70% moisture) has equal nutritive value to 1kgs of hay
  • Requires less storage and transport space 10 times less than hay.
  • Fermentation in silage reduces harmful nitrates accumulated in feeds.
  • Cost effective and increased income.
